What surprised you in code?

The P5 canvas doesn’t abide by external CSS rules, I think? So I had to change the CSS within the JavaScript file.

What structural decision became harder in HTML/CSS?

Making the drop down menus functional… For the sake of time, I likely will not be making them work completely.

What assumptions did the browser expose?

I always forget that my usual CSS margins (I have a default I like to use) won’t work once I add a header with a background.

What does your interface assume about layout?

It assumes that the “desktop” devices will be wide enough to support the two columns and the gutter, which are a set width.

What does CSS assume about flow?

That people will want navigation at the top of their screen.

What does HTML assume about hierarchy?

That people are okay with having all their links shown at once.

Where do those collide?

In this case, I guess my navigation can be very overwhelming since the CSS means that navigation is really in your face in two ways.
