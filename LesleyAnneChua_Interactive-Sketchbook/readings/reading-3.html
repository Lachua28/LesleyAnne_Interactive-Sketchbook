Reading Reflection: Shneiderman's 8 Golden Rules

Which rule feels most absent from your micro-project page?

#2 “Seek universal usability,” because my game is unplayable on any device that doesn’t have a keyboard. Since my game requires a key press to proceed to the next level or restart, you cannot progress through the game.

Which rule would most improve your sketchbook interface?

“#6 Permit easy reversal of actions.” Due to the lack of navigation on every page other than the index, there isn’t a way to navigate through every page unless you were to start on the landing page and press the browser back button between pages.

Which rule is hardest to implement honestly?

#5 “Prevent Errors” for the same reason in my previous reading reflection: you cannot predict every error that all your users may make, so you’d have to fix them overtime after observing common errors. For the sake of talking about something different, I think universal usability can be really difficult at times since you cannot please everyone. I’ve seen instances of disability-accommodating features in industrial design breaking other design rules, so it’s a matter of balancing. I’d assume there are similar issues in interactive design.

Did any rule contradict how you designed your system originally?

#6 (easy reversal of actions), since you have to restart the entire game if you fail a level; however, that’s intentional in my game to make it more difficult. Also, my game contradicts universal usability for the aforementioned key press reason.
