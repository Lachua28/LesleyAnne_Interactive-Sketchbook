Micro-Project: Self-Assessment

Core Rule & System Clarity: 20/20

I personally think I did a good job executing the rule of the user stopping the moving bus at a certain area (the bus stop) in order to "win." I think that the instructions written on the sketch make it straightforward enough to understand. In comparison to the "No Internet Dinosaur Game," I want to assume that the "level" count at the top right of the sketch implies that it is a game of sorts with a win condition.

State Progression: 15/15

I may be misunderstanding, but I believe the state progression present in my sketch is the additional "wrong" bus stop added after the first level. The addition of a wrong stop is a one-time change in the code structure, triggered after the player wins the first level. Other than that, I want to say that the main interaction (stopping the bus at the bus stop) is also tied to state progression because the "win" variable changes certain elements of the sketch.

Interaction & Evolution: 15/15

The main interaction of clicking the bus to stop it at the bus stop evolves the system by increasing the speed of the bus. The level counter increases by 1 every time the player stops the bus within the bus stop area, triggering the "win" variable. (The "win" variable resets when the player presses a key to continue to the next level.) Since the level counter is multiplied by the bus speed, it makes the bus exponentially faster after each win/level. Out of the keywords in the rubric, I consider this escalation.

Iterative Development: 15/15

Between Progress #1 & #2, there is the structural change of adding levels, a 'level reset' when you fail a level, and randomizing the position of the bus stop. Between Progress #2 & #3 (final), the structural change is adding the red "wrong stop" and improving the visuals. Additionally, I built this project on top of my Sketch #6, so you can see the biggest change between that and the final!

Documentation & Logging: 15/15

I want to say my documentation is clear enough because I wrote a lot about my thought process for progress #1 and then what I did for progress #2.

Risk & Experimentation: 10/10

I took my biggest risk/experimentation with trying to figure out movement and randomized positions for this project! Going into the project, I wasn't sure how to make automated movement, so I decided to do this micro-idea with the assumption that I would figure it out somewhere down the line. After figuring it out for progress #1, I experimented more with movement because I had to change the way I did it in order to create more levels. Originally, I did automated movement by adding 1 integer to the frameCount, but that only works for a single level. In hindsight, it was a simple fix (make my own counting variable), but it took time for me to realize that was the problem.

Participation & Critique Engagement: 9/10

The only part of this project that I think I fell short were critiques. I gave mostly surface-level critiques and didn't take many critiques for my project into consideration because I was mentally spent from figuring out the ideas I came up with by myself. I did add the suggestion of adding more bus stops (the wrong stop), but that's about it.
